What is our aim?

We provide estimates of the effective reproductive number, Re, based on longitudinal measurements of influenza RNA in wastewater. We also provide estimates based on laboratory-confirmed cases in each catchment for comparison.

How do we do it?

The effective reproductive number, Re, quantifies the expected number of people an infected individual will infect over time. Since infected individuals excrete influenza RNA into the sewer system (via saliva, feces, and/or sputum), wastewater samples reflect the number of people in a particular catchment area that are infected with influenza. These longitudinal measurements of influenza RNA in wastewater are then used to estimate Re. We use the R package EstimateR to estimate Re from the wastewater and case data. This method has previously been applied to SARS-CoV-2 in wastewater, those results are available here.
